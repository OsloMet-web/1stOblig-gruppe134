Introduction

Imagine that three years from now, you have finished your degree at OsloMet and have just been recruited to one of the largest consultancy firms in Norway. In the first week in your new position, you are told by your supervisor that, in addition to your individual tasks, you will be working with a small team of 5-6 people, who regularly meet to discuss and find good balanced solutions to complex situations and problems within the company or society in general.

Identify Situation

Cases that can benefit from different points of view are often more effectively solved in groups. Each team-member brings a different set of skills and experiences to the discussion and can help to shed light to the problem in different ways. Group discussions can also teach something new to members of the team that can help them solve similar problems in the future. Problems that are especially complex can also benefit from people with different expertise to solve different parts of the problem. While also easing the workload of the different team-members.
